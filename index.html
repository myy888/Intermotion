InterMotion: Human Motion Generation Interacted with Target Objects and Users in 3D Scenes

Yiyi Ma, Yachao Zhang, Xiu Li
Tsinghua University

Abstract

Natural and controllable human-scene interaction (HSI) generation assets are widely used in various fields such as VR/AR content creation and animation production. However, technology is still in its infancy, and existing methods rarely focus on the interaction with target objects and fine-grained controllability of text on actions. Therefore, we propose the InterMotion, a novel approach that enhances the motion interaction from two aspects: interacting with objects in 3D scenes and interacting with personal descriptions input by users. We propose Iterative Optimization Trajectory Decoder, which use the dual Transformer structure to iteratively learn the path planning and text-controlled human action sequences in the scene, so as to better embed the scene spatial correlation of the human action sequences. Then, based on the decoupling decoder, we add fine-grained motions in the scene reasonably to achieve text-based personalized human motion generation by introducing the text-to-motion model. Our extensive experiments and perceptual studies show that InterMotion can effectively generate pose-controllable human-scene interaction action sequences and outperform existing works based on generative model in terms of interaction accuracy and motion diversity.
